Forbes School

Designing a scalable instructional system for underserved learners.

CASE 4

Project Title: Forbes School

Organization / Industry: Independent Education Initiative • Language Education for Underserved Learners

Date: 2011–2021

1. Problem Statement

Traditional language programs fail underserved learners due to poor placement, rigid curricula, lack of differentiation, and weak progress tracking. A scalable, needs-driven instructional system was required.

2. My Role

End-to-End Instructional Designer • Curriculum Architect • Assessment Designer • LMS Implementation Lead

3. Target Audience

Children • Adults • Seniors • Immigrants • Low-income learners

4. Stakeholder & SME Alignment

  • Worked with educators as instructional SMEs to align curriculum pacing, assessment, and learner readiness.
  • Incorporated learner feedback as a primary stakeholder input into instructional iteration.
  • Balanced instructional quality with operational and resource constraints.

5. Key Constraints & Challenges

  • Multi-age cohorts
  • Diverse goals and pacing needs
  • Limited learner availability
  • Cultural and linguistic diversity
  • Resource constraints

6. Solution Overview

Designed a full instructional system including diagnostics, learner profiling, modular curricula, age-sensitive grouping, and LMS-based assessment and tracking. International standards were adapted to context and learner goals.

7. Instructional Design Strategies

  • Needs-first diagnostic design
  • Modular curriculum architecture
  • Scaffolded progression across ages
  • Continuous formative assessment
  • LMS-enabled transparency and accountability

8. Results / Impact

  • Improved retention through accurate placement
  • Measurable learner progress
  • High learner and stakeholder satisfaction
  • Scalable system across cohorts

9. Key Takeaway

Demonstrates systems-level instructional design integrating curriculum, assessment, and technology for diverse populations.
