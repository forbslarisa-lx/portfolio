Performance-oriented Growth Mindset course for professionals and people managers.

CASE 1

Project Title: Growth Mindset for Performance and Leadership

Organization / Industry: Corporate Client • Finance / Tech Solutions

Date: 4 weeks • Needs Analysis → Design → Development → Pilot Delivery

Solution Artifacts (isEazy): Growth Mindset eCourse

Solution Overview

Designed a performance-oriented Growth Mindset program reframing mindset as a business capability. The course emphasized observable workplace behaviors over theory, integrating realistic dialogue, coaching scripts, and actionable decision-making tools.

Problem Statement

Mid-level professionals and people managers demonstrated strong technical expertise but struggled with failure tolerance, feedback defensiveness, learning from mistakes, and blame-oriented post-mortems. These behaviors reduced collaboration, adaptability, and psychological safety. The business required a behavior-shifting solution, not awareness-level training.

My Role

Lead Instructional Designer & Learning Architect

Target Audience

Mid-level professionals and people managers • Client-facing, high-pressure roles • Limited formal leadership training

Stakeholder & SME Alignment

  • Collaborated with L&D leaders and people managers as domain SMEs to align behavioral concepts with existing performance and evaluation frameworks
  • Validated scenarios and language to ensure credibility for experienced professional learners
  • Balanced learner resistance, leadership expectations, and instructional clarity through iterative feedback cycles

Key Constraints & Challenges

  • Skepticism toward mindset-based learning
  • Resistance to discussing failure
  • Limited training time
  • High credibility threshold for experienced professionals

Learning Outcomes

  • Growth Mindset: Develop openness around mistakes, reframe feedback as constructive.
  • Performance-Oriented Feedback: Deliver actionable feedback and debrief effectively.

Instructional Design Strategies

  • Performance-Based Design: Focus on feedback behavior, response to failure, and team debrief practices.
  • Scenario-Based Learning: Realistic client-loss dialogue to surface implicit beliefs without moralizing.
  • Behavior Modeling: Professional, non-preachy language to maintain credibility.
  • Choice Architecture: Decision-based reflection exercises for transfer to practice.
  • Cognitive Load Control: Short, goal-driven content blocks with immediate relevance.

Assessment & Evaluation

  • Pre/post self-assessment
  • Scenario-based final evaluation
  • Facilitator observation
  • Qualitative participant feedback

Results / Impact

  • Increased openness around mistakes
  • Improved quality of debrief conversations
  • More constructive feedback reported by managers
  • Higher engagement than prior soft-skills programs

Key Takeaway

Demonstrates ability to translate behavioral science into credible, performance-aligned learning for experienced professionals.
