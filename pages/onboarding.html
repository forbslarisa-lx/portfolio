VividWorld Apparel Onboarding Program

A learner-centered experience that accelerates new hire readiness.

CASE 4

Project Title: VividWorld Apparel Onboarding Program

Organization / Industry: Independent Educational Initiative • Public Online Education

Instructional Designer: Larisa Forbs

Delivery Platform: Articulate Rise 360

Audience: New Customer Experience Specialists

1. Project Purpose

The purpose of this project is to design a learner-centered onboarding experience that accelerates new hire readiness during the first 30 days while supporting confidence, role clarity, and consistent customer experience behaviors. The program adopts a two-way onboarding model in which learners actively interpret expectations, connect personal strengths to the role, and practice decision-making in realistic workplace situations.

2. Instructional Philosophy: Two-Way Onboarding

This program treats onboarding as a mutual adaptation process between employee and organization rather than a one-directional information transfer.

  • Learner identity integration with organizational values
  • Psychological safety and normalization of early uncertainty
  • Reflection and self-assessment
  • Practice through realistic scenarios
  • Early confidence building
  • Clear visibility of support systems and growth pathways

3. Business Goals

  • Faster time-to-productivity
  • Consistent customer experience delivery
  • Improved communication and teamwork
  • Reduced early-stage uncertainty
  • Increased engagement and retention potential
  • Improved adherence to operational standards

4. Learning Goals

  • Describe the organization’s mission and values.
  • Explain the Customer Experience Specialist role and its impact.
  • Apply customer interaction strategies using the IMPACT framework.
  • Demonstrate understanding of workplace expectations and safety.
  • Identify support resources and growth opportunities.
  • Navigate the first 30 days with confidence.

5. Instructional Framework

The course architecture follows a progressive onboarding pathway: Context → Identity → Role → Practice → Application → Growth → Independence

6. IMPACT Customer Experience Framework

Element Description
I — Initial Welcome Creating a positive first impression and establishing connection.
M — Merchandise and Presentation Maintaining organized, appealing displays and brand consistency.
P — Product Availability Ensuring customers can locate desired products efficiently.
A — Assistance Providing proactive guidance and personalized support.
C — Checkout Experience Creating a smooth and positive purchase completion.
T — Teamwork Coordinating effectively with colleagues to support customer outcomes.

7. Course Structure Overview

  • Section 1 — Welcome to VividWorld Apparel
  • Section 2 — What We Do and Why It Matters
  • Section 3 — Our Family
  • Section 4 — How We Work
  • Section 5 — Growth, Rewards, and Community
  • Section 6 — Your First 30 Days
  • Section 7 — Tools, Resources, and Support

8. Interaction Strategy

Interaction design emphasizes active learning through scenarios, knowledge checks, reflection prompts, and applied decision-making. Frequent micro-interactions reduce cognitive load and support retention.

9. Learning Theory Foundations

  • Adult Learning Theory (Andragogy): Learners connect prior experience to new knowledge and focus on practical application.
  • Experiential Learning (Kolb): Scenarios provide concrete experiences followed by reflection and conceptual understanding.
  • Social Learning Theory (Bandura): Modeling of behaviors through examples and feedback supports skill acquisition.
  • Cognitive Load Theory: Information is chunked into manageable segments with progressive complexity.
  • Self-Efficacy Theory: Confidence-building interactions increase learner belief in their ability to succeed.

10. SME Collaboration

Subject Matter Experts support validation of operational accuracy, scenario realism, behavioral expectations, and assessment correctness. Iterative review cycles occur during storyboard development prior to course build.

11. Expected Outcomes

The program is intended to increase learner confidence, improve consistency of customer experience behaviors, strengthen readiness during the first month, and reduce early-stage uncertainty.
