Cultural Literacy Curricula Development

Bridging the gap between academically rigorous queer theory and accessible public value-driven education.

CASE 4

Project Title: Queerology

Organization / Industry: Independent Educational Initiative • Public Online Education

Date: 2023–2025

Solution Artifacts: Queerology Instagram

  • Webinar recordings
  • Presentation materials
  • Reading lists and session outlines

Solution Overview

Designed a live webinar-based learning format supported by curated academic materials. Sessions combined structured instruction, moderated discussion, and guided meaning-making.

Problem Statement

A gap existed between academically rigorous queer theory and accessible public education. Available resources were either inaccessible or oversimplified, limiting informed discourse and critical understanding.

My Role

Instructional Designer • Content Curator • Facilitator • Academic Translator

Target Audience

Adult learners without formal academic background • Educators, activists, journalists • International audience

Stakeholder & SME Alignment

  • Collaborated with faculty members and academic practitioners as primary subject matter experts
  • Co-developed course content with a colleague acting as SME, working as a small instructional design team rather than a solo designer
  • Navigated academic databases and peer-reviewed literature to identify, evaluate, and prioritize high-impact theoretical frameworks
  • Synthesized and distilled complex academic research into structured, teachable instructional narratives
  • Translated and adapted academic concepts into a target language in which no established academic resources currently exist, effectively creating a foundational reference base for future academic use
  • Balanced faculty expectations, theoretical rigor, learner accessibility, and psychological safety through iterative design and facilitation
  • Treated learners as key stakeholders, explicitly designing for access, equity, and cognitive load across diverse cultural and educational backgrounds

Key Constraints & Challenges

  • Translating theory without loss of meaning
  • Diverse cultural and knowledge baselines
  • Engagement with theory-heavy material
  • Psychological safety in sensitive discussions

Learning Outcomes

  • Academic grounding in gender studies, sociology, psychology
  • Scaffolded conceptual progression
  • Narrative-based instructional translation
  • Interactive reflection and Q&A
  • Explicit psychological safety framing

Assessment & Evaluation

  • Pre/post self-assessment
  • Scenario-based final evaluation
  • Facilitator observation
  • Qualitative participant feedback

Results / Impact

  • High live-session engagement
  • Strong qualitative feedback on clarity
  • Increased learner confidence discussing LGBTQ+ topics
  • Repeat attendance across sessions

Key Takeaway

Demonstrates academic translation and facilitation of complex theory for non-academic audiences.

CASE 5

Project Title: Us And Them. Understanding Xenophobia

Organization / Industry: Independent Educational Initiative • Public Online Education

Date: 2023–2025

Solution Artifacts: Us And Them: Understanding Xenophobia (Rise 360 Course)

  • Interactive learning modules
  • Reflection prompts
  • Discussion guides

Solution Overview

Designed an interactive, self-paced course focused on themes of perception, stereotypes, and inclusion, intended to engage participants in deep reflection on the roots and impacts of xenophobia. The course provides multiple entry points, including structured learning, reflection-driven activities, and guided dialogue for facilitated settings.

Problem Statement

There is a growing need for accessible, impactful educational resources that help individuals understand and confront xenophobia in a structured, thoughtful way. Many existing resources oversimplify or fail to engage learners deeply on this complex topic, limiting meaningful discourse and reflection.

My Role

Instructional Designer • Content Creator • Educational Facilitator

Target Audience

Teens and young adults • Educators • Social activists • International audience

Stakeholder & SME Alignment

  • Collaborated with subject matter experts in social psychology, sociology, and intercultural studies
  • Created content aligned with educational best practices in engaging diverse audiences on sensitive topics
  • Designed for accessibility and psychological safety, ensuring learners felt secure enough to engage with complex, emotionally challenging topics
  • Developed materials that bridge theory with real-world application, encouraging reflection on personal and societal attitudes towards outsiders

Key Constraints & Challenges

  • Addressing a sensitive topic while maintaining emotional safety for diverse learners
  • Ensuring accessibility for learners from a variety of cultural and educational backgrounds
  • Balancing depth of content with learner engagement and reflection
  • Adapting the material for different types of learners (self-paced vs. facilitated)

Learning Outcomes

  • Increased awareness of the psychological and social roots of xenophobia
  • Scaffolded learning journey that moves from reflection to deeper engagement
  • Interactive and reflective learning processes that encourage critical thinking
  • Explicit psychological safety framing to ensure a respectful learning environment

Assessment & Evaluation

  • Pre/post self-assessment to measure shifts in attitudes
  • Scenario-based reflection exercises to test understanding
  • Facilitator-led discussions (for group settings) to deepen understanding
  • Qualitative participant feedback to gauge impact on engagement and learning

Results / Impact

  • High engagement rates in live and self-paced learning modules
  • Positive feedback on course clarity and thought-provoking content
  • Increased confidence in discussing xenophobia and social inclusion
  • Repeat participation and strong demand for similar future topics

Key Takeaway

Shows how educational content can deeply engage learners in confronting complex societal issues, fostering both personal reflection and group dialogue.
